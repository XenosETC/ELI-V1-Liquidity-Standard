# **1. General Questions**

---

## **Q1. What is the ELI Standard?**

ELI (Eternal Liquidity Instrument) is a liquidity and collateral standard built for Ethereum Classic (ETC) and other immutable, Proof-of-Work chains. It uses **burned liquidity** (PoBL) and **immutable balance sheets** (PoBLBS) to create **zero-governance, permanent liquidity** that grows stronger over time.

---

## **Q2. Why does ETC need its own liquidity standard?**

Because ETC is:

* immutable
* non-governed
* PoW-secured
* scarce
* resistant to political influence

Ethereum-style liquidity models (treasuries, governance, migrations) contradict ETC’s philosophical foundation.
ELI restores **economic autonomy** and aligns DeFi with ETC’s identity.

---

## **Q3. What makes ELI different from normal DeFi?**

Traditional DeFi liquidity is:

* withdrawable
* migratable
* governed
* upgradeable
* treasury-controlled

ELI liquidity is:

* **permanent**
* **ungoverned**
* **unmigratable**
* **PoW-enforced**
* **time-reinforced**

It creates *hard liquidity* — a public good, not a political asset.

---

# **2. PoBL (Proof of Burn Liquidity)**

---

## **Q4. What is PoBL?**

PoBL is the process of **permanently burning LP tokens** so liquidity:

* cannot be withdrawn
* cannot be migrated
* cannot be altered
* cannot be politically controlled
* becomes infrastructure

It is the liquidity equivalent of “code is law.”

---

## **Q5. Why burn LP? Isn’t that risky?**

Burning LP eliminates:

* rug pulls
* treasury abuse
* governance exploits
* migration failures
* multisig corruption
* political capture

The only “risk” is that the project commits fully.
The tradeoff is **total user safety** and **unbreakable liquidity**.

---

## **Q6. Can teams keep some LP?**

Yes — but ANY liquidity designated as “ELI-compliant” **must** be burned.

Teams can maintain separate flexible liquidity **outside** the ELI base, but the foundation must be immutable.

# **3. PoBLBS (Balance Sheets)**

---

## **Q7. What is a PoBLBS?**

A **Proof of Burn Liquidity Balance Sheet** is an on-chain record showing:

* how much ETC is permanently locked
* how much paired asset is locked
* total LP supply burned
* minimum liquidity depth
* long-term backing strength

It is a transparent, immutable form of collateral accounting.

---

## **Q8. Why is this important?**

Because DeFi has never had:

* non-political collateral
* permanent reserves
* guaranteed liquidity floors
* immutable token backing

PoBLBS introduces this for the first time.

---

## **Q9. Can PoBLBS be used for lending or collateralization?**

Yes — that is one of the main purposes.
ELI creates **hard collateral**, which can power:

* trustless loans
* stable instruments
* liquidity-backed credit
* risk-minimized leverage
* miner-secured vaults

It is the first collateral model based entirely on burned reserves.

# **4. ZGF (Zero Governance Finance)**

---

## **Q10. What is ZGF?**

Zero Governance Finance is DeFi where:

* no multisigs exist
* no treasuries manage liquidity
* no DAOs vote on economics
* no parameters can be changed

It is NOT decentralized governance —
it is **no governance**.

---

## **Q11. Why no governance?**

Because governance:

* creates political attack surfaces
* enables corruption
* introduces biases
* contradicts immutability
* destabilizes liquidity

ELI’s logic is simple:

> **If no one controls liquidity, no one can corrupt it.**

---

## **Q12. Does ZGF mean devs have no role?**

Developers still build:

* tokenomics
* dApps
* interfaces
* tools
* extensions

But they cannot **control** the liquidity base.

They steward — not govern.

# **5. ELI as an Asset Class**

---

## **Q13. What exactly is an ELI asset?**

An ELI asset is any token or protocol that:

* burns its liquidity permanently
* publishes PoBLBS data
* uses tokenomics that respect the LP
* removes governance over its base liquidity

This forms a new asset class:

> **Immutable Collateral Instruments**

---

## **Q14. Why create a new asset class?**

Because:

* no existing financial model uses immutable liquidity
* no DeFi system provides incorruptible reserves
* no chain besides ETC can support this architecture
* no asset class is backed by permanent PoW-secured LP

ELI is fundamentally different from tokens, stablecoins, or governance assets.

---

## **Q15. Can ELI assets exist on other chains?**

Not properly.

PoS chains rely on:

* governance
* reversible economics
* treasury-controlled liquidity
* upgrade pipelines

ELI requires:

* immutability
* PoW finality
* neutral economics
* irreversible timechain effects

Only ETC can provide that foundation.

# **6. Tokenomics & Supply**

---

## **Q16. Can ELI assets have inflation or emissions?**

Yes — but:

* emissions must be mathematically sustainable
* supply increases cannot undermine LP strength
* rewards should not destabilize token value
* inflation must be predictable

ELI is not anti-inflation.
It is anti-exploitation.

---

## **Q17. What happens if tokenomics are bad?**

If emissions destroy market integrity, the token may fail — **but the liquidity cannot**.

The burned LP remains forever, ensuring:

* price floors
* collateral strength
* minimum depth
* market continuity

ELI guarantees liquidity permanence even if the project team disappears.

---

## **Q18. What if someone tries to game the system?**

They cannot alter the base LP.
They cannot take liquidity.
They cannot move liquidity.
They cannot govern liquidity.

Bad actors can damage their own token but **not** the ELI base.

---

They:

1. Launch their token or project.
2. Create liquidity.
3. Burn the LP permanently.
4. Publish PoBLBS data.
5. Document sustainable tokenomics.
6. Declare ELI-V1 compliance.

That’s it.

---

## **Q23. Can ELI support lending, staking, or vaults?**

Yes — as long as:

* none of those control the base liquidity
* tokenomics do not undermine the LP
* features layer on top of immutable reserves

ELI is a foundation — not a limitation.

---

## **Q24. What about stablecoins?**

Stablecoins can use ELI if:

* their primary backing comes from PoBLBS
* they do not require governance to maintain stability

This would create the first **immutable-backed stable primitives** in crypto.
